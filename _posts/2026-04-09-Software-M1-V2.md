This document defines the Software Requirements Specification for **Team M1**, responsible for the **Patient Mobile Application** within the Monitor Layer of the Limb Motion Recognition and Assistant system.

The purpose of this document is to:

<div style="margin-left: 2rem;">

<li>Align M1's functional and non-functional requirements with the rest of the system.</li>
</ul>
</div>

This document is intended to be read together with the system-level SRS. M1 does not redefine system-wide use cases; it specialises and expands them for the mobile patient context.

### 0.2 Team M1 Scope

**In scope for M1:**

<div style="margin-left: 2rem;">
<ul style="list-style: disc; margin: 0.5rem 0;">
<li>Real-time joint motion visualisation during exercise.</li>
<li>Posture correction alerts and haptic feedback during sessions.</li>
<li>Rehabilitation session management (start, pause, end).</li>
<li>Bluetooth/BLE sensor connection and status management from within the app.</li>
<li>Progress history and session records display.</li>
<li>Push notifications for session reminders and progress milestones.</li>
<li>Consumption of the IF2 API contract (WebSocket for real-time data, REST for historical data).</li>
<li>Co-ownership of the IF2 interface specification with Team V2.</li>
</ul>
</div>

**Out of scope for M1:**

<div style="margin-left: 2rem;">
<ul style="list-style: disc; margin: 0.5rem 0;">
<li>Sensor firmware and hardware (Teams S1 and S2).</li>
<li>AI inference and motion recognition logic (Team V1).</li>
<li>API gateway, database storage, and authentication backend (Team V2).</li>
<li>Clinical web dashboard for doctors and administrators (Team M2).</li>
<li>Prescription of exercise plans — plans are created by doctors via M2 and consumed by M1.</li>
</ul>
</div>

    <dd>V2 → M1 (server-push). M1 sends only heartbeat pings.</dd>
  </div>
  <div class="ws-spec-item">
    <dt>Target Frequency</dt>
    <dd>One payload per recognised motion frame (~100 ms).</dd>
  </div>
  <div class="ws-spec-item">
    <dt>Connection Timeout</dt>
    <dd>Server must keep connection alive during brief packet gaps. M1 must retry on drop.</dd>
  </div>
  <div class="ws-spec-item">
    <dt>Reconnection Strategy</dt>
    <dd>Exponential backoff — max 3 retries, then surface error to patient.</dd>
  </div>
</div>

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements

### 5.1 Performance and Latency

<div class="nfr-grid">
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-01</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">Real-time visualisation must update within <strong>200ms</strong> of each incoming WebSocket frame to maintain perceived immediacy.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-02</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">Posture alerts must be displayed within <strong>100ms</strong> of receiving an alert payload.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-03</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">App launch to login screen must take less than <strong>3 seconds</strong> on a mid-range device.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-04</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">REST calls must have a client-side timeout of <strong>10 seconds</strong> with a clear error message on expiry.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-05</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">The 3D rendering pipeline must maintain at least <strong>30 frames per second</strong> on the target device class.</div>
  </div>
</div>

### 5.2 Usability

<div class="nfr-grid">
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-06</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">The app must be usable by patients with <strong>no prior technical knowledge</strong>. No technical terminology shall be exposed in the patient-facing UI.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-07</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">All critical actions (start session, end session, connect sensor) must be reachable within <strong>2 taps</strong> from the dashboard.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-08</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">Posture correction messages must be written in plain language: e.g., <em>"Bend your knee more"</em> rather than <em>"Deviation: -24.8°"</em>.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-09</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">Font sizes and contrast ratios must meet <strong>WCAG AA</strong> accessibility standards.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-10</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">Error messages must always offer a next step. <strong>No dead-end error states</strong> permitted.</div>
  </div>
</div>

### 5.3 Security

<div class="nfr-grid">
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-11</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">All REST communication must use <strong>HTTPS with TLS 1.2 or higher</strong>.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-12</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">All WebSocket communication must use <strong>WSS (encrypted)</strong>.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-13</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">JWT tokens must be stored using platform secure storage (<strong>iOS Keychain / Android Keystore</strong>).</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-14</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">No biometric or health data may be <strong>logged to device console or crash logs</strong>.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-15</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">The app must comply with <strong>GDPR</strong> requirements for health data. Patients must consent before data collection begins.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="nfr-card">
    <div class="nfr-id">NFR-M1-16</div>
    <div class="nfr-text">Session tokens must be <strong>invalidated on logout</strong>.</div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="nfr-grid">
